Data Sources

Historical GDP data attribution, coverage gaps, and known statistical outliers

This page describes the built-in demo dataset only — GDP Trajectories. It does not apply to datasets you import.

Primary sources

SourceCoverageUsed for
IMF World Economic Outlook (WEO)1980–2025, 190+ countriesPrimary historical GDP series; short-run projections through 2029

Country coverage

The simulator covers 100 individual countries plus four regional aggregates (World, G7, G20, EU). The cutoff for inclusion was a minimum of 10 years of continuous historical data. Small island states and territories with populations below 1 million are excluded.

Known statistical outliers

The following data points are flagged in Power BI exports as IsOutlier = true. They appear in the historical series but should be interpreted with caution.

Country / YearAnomaly
Ireland 2015 OUTLIER +25.2% GDP artefact from multinational corporate restructuring (MNC intellectual property relocation). The Irish CSO introduced modified GNI* (GNI*) in 2017 to address this. Standard GDP figures dramatically overstate Irish domestic economic activity for this year.
Ireland 2014 OUTLIER Precursor balance-sheet reclassification from the same MNC activity.
Libya 2012 OUTLIER Post-civil-war statistical base effect. Production collapsed in 2011 (Gaddafi-era civil conflict) and rebounded sharply in 2012, producing an unusually high nominal growth rate that does not reflect real economic reconstruction.
Qatar 1993 OUTLIER Early LNG export ramp-up produces a very high base-effect growth rate that is not representative of the underlying economy's maturity level.

Projection horizon (2026–2100)

Beyond 2025, all values are model projections. They are not IMF or World Bank forecasts. The underlying growth rate assumptions are calibrated to long-run consensus (see Calibration), but the further from 2025, the more uncertain the output.

Update cadence

Historical data is updated annually, following the April IMF WEO release. No automated live feed is used — updates are manual and versioned in the repository.
