Fixed Constraints

Hard-coded milestones that scenarios and user inputs cannot override

This page describes the built-in demo dataset only — GDP Trajectories. It does not apply to datasets you import.

Certain long-range trajectories in the model are locked to their calibrated values and cannot be altered by scenarios, scope overrides, or user-imported data. These constraints reflect our best calibration of historically-anchored convergence dynamics for the three largest economies by 2100.

These values are not political projections. They are calibrated extrapolations of long-run trend growth rates anchored to 1990–2025 historical data. Disagreement with these values should be directed to the calibration discussion in the GitHub repository.

China overtakes USA

China's GDP crosses the United States baseline in approximately 2037. This crossing point is fixed. Scenarios affect the magnitude and timing of the gap that opens up after the crossing, but the crossing itself cannot be removed.

Why these are locked

The long-run order and approximate magnitudes of these three economies are robust outputs of any reasonable conditional-convergence model given:

  • India's 1.4 billion population and current ~6–7% real growth trajectory
  • China's 1.4 billion population and maturation from a middle-income to high-income economy over the next 40 years
  • The USA's stable 2–2.5% long-run real growth rate and ongoing nominal USD anchor role

Moving these numbers arbitrarily would misrepresent the economic consensus and undermine the model's educational value. If you believe the calibration is wrong, please open an issue on GitHub with supporting evidence.

What scenarios CAN change

Scenarios adjust growth rates over a window, which shifts the GDP path around the fixed milestones. They can make India reach $95T earlier or later, change how large China's lead over the USA is, and alter intermediate ranks (e.g. Germany vs Indonesia in 2050). What they cannot do is swap the top-3 order at 2100 or move the China/USA crossover by more than a few years.
