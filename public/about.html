What this is

Data ISC is an open-source, browser-based simulation engine. Import any time-series dataset — upload a CSV or XLSX file and the tool automatically detects its structure, visualises the data, and lets you define what-if scenarios to see how outcomes diverge under different assumptions.

Connect an AI provider of your choice to power intelligent scenario analysis: the model reads your data and your plain-language description of a scenario, then computes per-entity impact estimates with causal reasoning — which entities are affected, by how much, and over what time horizon.

Built-in demo: GDP Trajectories

The tool ships with a built-in data model covering GDP trajectories for 100+ countries from 1990 to 2100. The following applies specifically to this demo dataset — not to imported datasets, which follow their own structure.

The GDP model runs a deterministic projection engine calibrated to historical data from the IMF World Economic Outlook, World Bank national accounts, and UN Statistics Division. Growth rates are derived from long-run convergence theory, demographic projections, and productivity trends. All values are nominal USD — no PPP adjustments, no inflation deflators. The 2100 figures should be read as consistent extrapolations of calibrated trends, not predictions; structural breaks and technological discontinuities are not modelled.

Design principles

🖥

Model stays server-side

The engine logic and calibration data are never sent to the browser. The client receives only computed outputs.

💵

Nominal USD only

All GDP figures are current nominal US dollars. PPP conversions and inflation adjustments are explicitly out of scope.

📡

No tracking

The tool runs entirely in your browser. No analytics, no telemetry, no cookies. Your data and your AI provider key never leave your machine.

🏁

Bar Chart Race

Animated ranking race from 1990 to 2100 — or use your own dataset. Scenario effects apply live: watch how a pandemic or automation wave reshapes rankings in real time. Smart callouts highlight leader changes and landmark milestones automatically.

⑂

Scenario branching (GitDiff)

Branch the baseline into two rival futures — say, high automation vs severe demographic strain — and watch the two timelines diverge on a single graph. A shaded divergence band and live delta readout show the gap widening or narrowing year by year.

📤

Social video export

Export the animated race as a WebM video in 16:9, 1:1, or 9:16 format — ready to post on LinkedIn, Instagram, or TikTok without any post-production editing.

⚡️

Export & BI connectors

Export to CSV, JSON, or a Power BI-ready ZIP (star schema: fact table + dimension tables). A native Power BI connector for DirectQuery is on the roadmap.

Limitations

This is a projection model, not a forecast. The further out the horizon, the less predictive power any model has. The 2100 figures should be read as consistent extrapolations of calibrated long-run trends, not predictions. Structural breaks, wars, pandemics, and technological discontinuities are not modelled.
