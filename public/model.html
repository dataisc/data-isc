Bring your own data

Data ISC is data-source agnostic. Upload any CSV or XLSX file with time-series data and the engine automatically detects its structure — which column is the entity, which is the time axis, which columns are values. No fixed schema is required beyond that.

Any domain works: economic indicators, environmental measurements, clinical trial progressions, sales figures, energy output — if it has entities evolving over time, the tool can visualise and simulate it.

Supported input formats: CSV, XLSX, JSON (simulator export). Copy-paste from a spreadsheet also works via the import panel.

No automatic database connections in this release. Data must be uploaded as a file. Direct connectors to SQL databases, REST APIs, or cloud data warehouses (BigQuery, Snowflake, Databricks) are planned for a future release.

For imported datasets the engine follows the same general logic as the GDP demo — year-by-year projections along each entity's detected trend, with what-if scenarios applied as adjustments over the horizon you define. The specific calibration, growth-rate assumptions, and fixed milestones documented in the sub-pages below apply only to the built-in GDP model and will not carry over to your data.

Demo model: GDP Trajectories

The built-in demo uses a deterministic, annual-step projection engine calibrated to historical IMF GDP data. It extends every country's trajectory from 2025 to 2100 using long-run growth rates that slow as economies mature (conditional convergence). Scenario shocks apply multiplicative adjustments over a user-specified window, then decay back toward baseline. All values are nominal USD — no PPP, no inflation deflators.

The sub-pages below document this model's methodology, data sources, calibration, and fixed constraints. These details apply specifically to the GDP demo dataset, not to user-imported data.

Visualisation modes

Once data is loaded — either the built-in GDP model or your own upload — the engine offers four chart modes:

  • Map view — choropleth world map, scrub through time with the year slider.
  • Top 10 chart — ranked bar chart at a selected year.
  • Trajectories — multi-entity line chart from start year to 2100. Includes a ⑂ Branch (GitDiff) mode: split the baseline into two rival futures (e.g. high automation vs demographic strain), each its own stack of scenarios, and watch the two lines diverge on one graph with a shaded divergence band and a live gap readout.
  • Bar Chart Race — animated ranking race. Scenario effects apply live, reshaping trajectories from the scenario start year onward. Automated callouts surface leader changes (🏆) and landmark milestones (🎯) so viewers don't miss critical moments. Export the animation as a 16:9, 1:1, or 9:16 video for social media.

What the engine does not do

The engine produces internally consistent projections, not forecasts. For user datasets, what-if scenarios are powered by AI reasoning (if a provider is connected) or simple compound growth adjustments. The engine does not model causal mechanisms on its own — that reasoning is delegated to the AI layer.

Server-side boundary

The GDP model engine (models/gdp-trajectories/compute.js) and its calibration data run exclusively on the server. The browser receives only computed output arrays — never growth rate tables, scenario parameters, or calibration coefficients. User-imported data is processed entirely client-side and never sent to the server.

Transparency

The full source is published under AGPLv3 at GitHub. The methodology pages below document the GDP model in enough detail for independent verification.
