🇪🇺 EU Federal Integration

Deep fiscal union and harmonised economic governance across EU member states

Type
Policy scenario
Scope
EU member states (27 countries)
Severity
Moderate positive
Duration
Permanent structural (no decay)

The premise

This scenario models a hypothetical EU-wide move toward deep fiscal federalism: a common EU treasury issuing Eurobonds, shared fiscal stabilisers, harmonised corporate tax floors, and a single capital markets union. The political framing is 2027–2035 ratification of major treaty changes following a hypothetical crisis that breaks the unanimity deadlock.

Expected effects

  • Core eurozone (Germany, France, Netherlands, Belgium, Austria): modest positive boost from deeper capital market integration and reduced borrowing costs for sovereign debt.
  • Periphery and Eastern members (Italy, Spain, Portugal, Greece, Poland, Romania): larger gains from access to pooled fiscal capacity, convergence funding, and reduced spreads.
  • Non-eurozone EU members (Sweden, Denmark, Czech Republic, Hungary): smaller gains — they participate in internal market deepening but not fiscal union.

What this scenario does not model

Political transition costs, institutional friction, creditor-country resistance, or the possibility that fiscal union fails to materialise or is reversed. It is a best-case structural integration scenario, not a probabilistic forecast.

How to run this in the simulator

Open the simulator, scroll to the Scenarios panel, and select EU Federal Integration from the Policy tab. Choose a start year (2027–2035 is most plausible) and observe the divergence in European GDP trajectories against the baseline in the Top 10 and Trajectories charts.
