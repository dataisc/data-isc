🌱 Global Carbon Tax

A $100/tonne global carbon price applied uniformly from 2028

Type
Policy scenario (green transition)
Scope
Global (all 100 countries)
Severity
Moderate negative (near-term) / Neutral to positive (long-run)
Duration
Permanent — negative drag fades as economies decarbonise

The premise

All countries adopt a harmonised carbon price of $100 per tonne of CO₂ equivalent from 2028. Revenue recycling (dividend payments, green investment) is assumed to offset about half the GDP drag. The carbon price rises at 3%/year in real terms. By 2060, most economies have substantially decarbonised and the carbon cost drag fades.

Near-term effects (2028–2040)

  • Heavy fossil fuel exporters (Saudi Arabia, Russia, Nigeria, Iraq, Kuwait): largest negative impact — revenue loss from reduced fossil fuel demand and price pressure.
  • Carbon-intensive manufacturers (China, India, Indonesia, Poland): near-term growth headwind as energy and industrial costs rise.
  • Advanced economies with diversified energy: smaller negative impact; faster transition to renewables limits the drag.

Long-run effects (2040–2100)

  • As decarbonisation completes, the carbon cost drag disappears.
  • Countries that invested carbon revenue in green infrastructure see a productivity dividend.
  • Physical climate risk avoided (not explicitly modelled) represents a large counterfactual benefit not captured in these projections.

What this scenario does not capture

The avoided cost of climate change — damage to infrastructure, agriculture, health, and coastal flooding — is not in scope for this GDP projection model. The scenario shows only the direct growth rate effect of the carbon price, not the counterfactual damages avoided. In reality, a well-designed carbon tax is likely net positive once avoided climate costs are included.

How to run this in the simulator

Open the simulator and select Global Carbon Tax from the Scenarios panel. The Scenario Impact chart shows which countries face the largest near-term GDP cost. Combine with the Green Energy Transition scenario to see the full decarbonisation pathway.
