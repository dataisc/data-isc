🌐 Global Free Trade

Complete elimination of tariffs, quotas, and trade barriers worldwide

Type
Policy scenario
Scope
Global (all 100 countries)
Severity
Moderate positive
Duration
Permanent structural (no decay)

The premise

A hypothetical WTO Doha-successor agreement that eliminates all goods tariffs, substantially reduces services trade barriers, and harmonises customs procedures globally. Implementation is modelled as beginning in 2028 with a 5-year phase-in.

Expected effects

  • Export-oriented developing economies (Vietnam, Bangladesh, Ethiopia, Ghana): largest gains in percentage terms — trade access accelerates manufacturing-led growth.
  • Large emerging markets (India, Indonesia, Mexico, Brazil): significant gains from both export expansion and import competition forcing productivity improvements.
  • Advanced economies: smaller percentage gains but large in absolute dollar terms. Services trade liberalisation is particularly important for the USA and UK.
  • Commodity exporters (Saudi Arabia, Nigeria, Angola): mixed — terms of trade may shift as manufacturing costs fall and commodity demand responds.

Caveats

The model captures aggregate GDP effects. Distributional effects within countries (which sectors win, which workers are displaced) are not modelled. The political economy of reaching and sustaining such an agreement is also absent — this is a structural thought experiment, not a policy forecast.

How to run this in the simulator

Open the simulator, scroll to the Scenarios panel, and select Global Free Trade. The Scenario Impact chart (third tab) shows which countries gain most relative to baseline at any horizon year.
