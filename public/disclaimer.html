Important limitations on the use of simulator outputs

Data ISC produces illustrative projections, not forecasts. All outputs are the result of a deterministic model with explicit, simplified assumptions. They should not be used as the sole basis for financial, investment, policy, or business decisions.

1. Not a forecast

The projections generated by this simulator are model outputs based on calibrated trend extrapolation. They are not macroeconomic forecasts and should not be cited as such. The IMF, World Bank, OECD, and other authoritative institutions publish regularly updated economic forecasts that are better suited for near-term policy or investment analysis.

2. Nominal USD only

All GDP figures are in nominal US dollars. They are not inflation-adjusted, not PPP-adjusted, and do not account for future exchange rate movements. Comparing nominal USD GDP across decades contains an embedded inflation and exchange-rate component that must be accounted for in any serious analysis.

3. Structural breaks and tail risks are not modelled

The model does not represent:

  • Wars, geopolitical fragmentation, or state collapse
  • Pandemics, natural disasters, or climate tipping points
  • Technological discontinuities (artificial general intelligence, fusion energy, etc.)
  • Financial crises, sovereign defaults, or currency collapses
  • Demographic reversals or migration shocks

Real-world economic trajectories will diverge from model projections due to these and other unpredictable events.

4. Long-horizon uncertainty

Projection uncertainty grows non-linearly with the time horizon. GDP projections at a 75-year horizon (2100) carry an uncertainty range that is orders of magnitude wider than the central estimate. They are best interpreted as directional indicators of relative economic scale, not precise quantities.

5. No investment or financial advice

Nothing in this simulator constitutes investment, financial, legal, or professional advice. Decisions with material financial consequences should be made with the guidance of qualified professionals using the most current data available.

6. Data accuracy

Historical data is sourced from IMF, World Bank, and UN databases, and is subject to the data quality limitations and subsequent revisions of those sources. Known statistical outliers are documented in Data Sources.

7. Scenario mechanics

Scenario effects are simplified representations of complex policy dynamics. They use additive growth rate adjustments that do not capture non-linearities, second-order effects, or policy implementation risks. Two scenarios stacked together produce additive effects, which is a significant simplification.
